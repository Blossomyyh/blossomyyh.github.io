INTRODUCTION

Prefix

This is Blossom Yin.
OK.
Let’s begin with my name

First, please look at these magnificent scenes. We call these cheery trees, also, the flower is called——
Guess what? ——Cherry blossom!
Obviously, my name-blossom-is derived from that owning to a figure in one Japanese comic strip
Her name is    s a k u r a -  the same meaning as cherry blossom.
I am deeply admired by her perseverance, self-assuredness, and the unpretentious elegance.

In the comic stripe, a world view is elaborated that

anything is inevitable
which becomes my motto.
Namely, no matter what happens, joyful or sorrowful, it always comes for a reason but not by chance or accident. Though so cruel the reality was, you had to admit, as the proverb goes You reap what you sow.
That is, whatever you recognize or not, it is there waiting for you. (And that is why)
However, I learn something else from that comic which help me turn a positive attitude towards life.
The  b e l i e f
The belief in yourself, in human natural senses, in the promising future, in the power from your interior which could totally overturn the world.
So many people succeeded, right?  Why not me?  Why not you?

Those characters make me think and ponder but not indulge in the dazzling information on digital gadgets.
It’s my great pleasure to reading, designing, drawing and playing the piano.
Sometimes a bit lonely, so I also prefer to relating to others.
I cherish the ‘yuan’ in Chinese character-surprisingly there is no word the same meaning world!
I think it is a mysterious experience to meet everybody!
So that I like taking part in some organizations like international department of students’ union, where I meet different people from Taiwan, Germany, France…
And thank you guys for listening to my story, and what about yours?

The living organism, in a situation determined by the play of energy on the surface of the globe, ordinarily receives more energy than is necessary for maintaining life; the excess energy (wealth) can be used for the growth of a system (e.g., an organism); if the system can no longer grow, or if the excess cannot be completely absorbed in its growth, it must necessarily be lost without profit; it must be spent, willingly or not, gloriously or catastrophically (Bataille Share I, 21).
