INTRODUCTION

Prefix

This is Blossom Yin.
OK. Let’s begin with my name.

First, please look at these magnificent scenes. We call these cheery trees, also, the flower is called——Guess what? ——Cherry blossom!
Obviously, my name-blossom-is derived from that owning to a figure in one Japanese comic strip-Her name is sakula-the same meaning as cherry blossom. I am deeply admired by the perseverance, self-assuredness, and her unpretentious elegance .
Don’t you think her smile is devastating? Until now, I still feel honored to come across such a beauty.
In the comic stripe, a world view is elaborated that everything is inevitable, which becomes my motto.
Namely, no matter what happens, joyful or sorrowful, it always comes for a reason but not by chance or accident. Well, some of you may totally disagree with me.
But I think almost everyone have encountered the following situation that you got a terrible credit which beyond your expectations, then you would always find something you had disregarded contributed to that result.
Though so cruel the reality was, you had to admit, as the proverb goes ’You reap what you sow’. That is, whatever you recognize or not, it is there waiting for you. (And that is why)
By now, you probably defined me as a pessimist.
However, actually, I learn something else from that comic which help me turn a positive attitude towards life.
The belief.
The belief in yourself, in human natural senses, in the promising future, in the power from your interior which could totally overturn the world. So many people succeeded, right? Why not me? Why not you? Those characters make me think and ponder but not indulge in the dazzling information on digital gadgets. It’s my great pleasure to reading, designing, drawing and playing the piano. Sometimes a bit lonely, so I also prefer to relating to others. I cherish the ‘yuan’ in Chinese character-surprisingly there is no word the same meaning world! I think it is a mysterious experience to meet everybody! So that I like taking part in some organizations like international department of students’ union, where I meet different people from Taiwan, Germany, France… well, I strongly recommend our organization—Buddy Program to you! I’m one of the morganizer and tonight is our first ice breaking party!
